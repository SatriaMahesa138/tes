Padang Padang Beach

Padang-padang Beach is a beach in southern Bali characterized by cliffs with white and fine sand. Padang-padang Beach is at the westernmost tip of the Uluwatu Peninsula, approximately an hour's drive from Denpasar.

Suluban Beach

The strategic location of Suluban Beach is flanked by Uluwatu Temple and Padang-Padang or Labuan Sait Beach. Suluban Beach is also known as Blue Point Beach, especially by foreign tourists.

Uluwatu Temple

This temple, which is located at the southwest tip of the island of Bali on a steep and high rock platform jutting out into the sea, is the Sad Kayangan Temple which is believed by Hindus to be a buffer from the 9 winds. This temple was originally used as a place to worship a holy priest from the 11th century named Empu Kuturan. He handed down the teachings of the Traditional Village with all its rules.

Kecak Dance With Sunset View

See firsthand the Kecak dance, a typical Balinese dance that tells the story of the Ramayana. Accompanied by a beautiful sunset.
